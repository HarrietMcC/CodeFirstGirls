The United States of America

Nevada

Las Vegas

Las Vegas is an internationally renowned major resort city, known primarily for its gambling, shopping, fine dining, entertainment, and nightlife. Try your hand with Lady Luck at Ceasar's Palace Casino and then take all of your winnings to one of the many Premium Shopping Outlets across the city. Then head to a fan-favourite Magic Mike show before dancing the night away at Hakkasan Nightclub, hosted by world-famous DJs and artists.

Las Vegas, Nevada

New York

The Big Apple

The city so great they named it twice! New York City is bursting with diversity and excitement – from the iconic attractions you've seen on TV and in the movies to hidden gems waiting to be uncovered. We recommend spending the majority of your time here sightseeing as there is plenty to do here without breaking your budget, although be prepared to encounter hefty crowds. Fan-favourites include the Statue of Liberty, Timesquare, Central Park and the Empire State Building. Also, if not for taste then for amusement's sake, look out for a cup of tea "the English way", served with warm milk - who knew?!

New York, New York

California

Los Angeles

The City of Angels is known for its Mediterranean-like climate, ethnic diversity, Hollywood, the entertainment industry, and its sprawling metropolis. Be sure to spend your time here basking in the year-round sun. Santa Monica Pier is always a win day or night. If hikes are your thing, Griffith Observatory is not to be missed. But you can't leave LA without a taste of celebrity, be sure to check out the Hollywood sign or maybe even take a celeb-spotting tour with one of the city's many companies.

Hollywood, LA
